Various Portents

By Alice Oswald

Various stars. Various kings.<br/> Various sunsets, signs, cursory insights.<br/> Many minute attentions, many knowledgeable watchers,<br/> Much cold, much overbearing darkness.<br/>

Various long midwinter Glooms.<br/> Various Solitary and Terrible Stars.<br/> Many Frosty Nights, many previously Unseen Sky-flowers.<br/> Many people setting out (some of them kings) all clutching at stars.<br/>

More than one North Star, more than one South Star.<br/> Several billion elliptical galaxies, bubble nebulae, binary systems,<br/> Various dust lanes, various routes through varying thicknesses of Dark,<br/> Many tunnels into deep space, minds going back and forth.<br/>

Many visions, many digitally enhanced heavens,<br/> All kinds of glistenings being gathered into telescopes:<br/> Fireworks, gasworks, white-streaked works of Dusk,<br/> Works of wonder and/or water, snowflakes, stars of frost . . .<br/>

Various dazed astronomers dilating their eyes,<br/> Various astronauts setting out into laughterless earthlessness,<br/> Various 5,000-year-old moon maps,<br/> Various blindmen feeling across the heavens in braille.<br/>

Various gods making beautiful works in bronze,<br/> Brooches, crowns, triangles, cups and chains,<br/> And all sorts of drystone stars put together without mortar.<br/> Many Wisemen remarking the irregular weather.<br/>

Many exile energies, many low-voiced followers,<br/> Watches of wisp of various glowing spindles,<br/> Soothsayers, hunters in the High Country of the Zodiac,<br/> Seafarers tossing, tied to a star . . .<br/>

Various people coming home (some of them kings). Various headlights.<br/> Two or three children standing or sitting on the low wall.<br/> Various winds, the Sea Wind, the sound-laden Winds of Evening<br/> Blowing the stars towards them, bringing snow.<br/>
